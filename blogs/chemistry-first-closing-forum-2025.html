Celebrating Excellence Chemistry First Closing Forum 2025

Join us for an inspiring celebration of scientific achievement as we honor the exceptional work of our graduating students and faculty members at the Department of Chemistry, Faculty of Science.

It is with immense pride and excitement that the Department of Chemistry at the Faculty of Science announces the inaugural Chemistry First Closing Forum (CFCF–01) for the academic year 2024–2025. This landmark event represents our commitment to celebrating scientific excellence and recognizing the outstanding achievements of our academic community.

"Excellence in science is not just about discovery—it's about inspiring the next generation of researchers to push the boundaries of knowledge and innovation."

Dr. Mahmoud Basseem, Assistant Professor of Applied Organic Chemistry

Event Details

Date

Sunday, June 22, 2025

Venue

Department of Chemistry
Faculty of Science, Al-Azhar University

This exceptional forum will serve as a platform to showcase the remarkable achievements of our graduating students and celebrate the academic milestones reached by our esteemed faculty members. The event embodies our department's dedication to fostering a culture of scientific excellence and academic recognition.

Program Highlights

Scientific Seminars by Graduating Students

Our exceptional senior undergraduate students will present their graduation projects, demonstrating the culmination of years of dedicated study and research. These presentations will showcase innovative research methodologies, groundbreaking findings, and the practical applications of their work in the field of chemistry.

  • Original research project presentations
  • Q&A sessions with faculty and peers
  • Poster session displaying student research
  • Interactive demonstrations of experimental techniques

Recognition of Academic Excellence

We will honor the outstanding students who have demonstrated exceptional performance in the research project course. This recognition celebrates not only their academic achievements but also their dedication to advancing scientific knowledge and their potential to become future leaders in chemistry.

  • Awards for top-performing research projects
  • Recognition of innovative methodologies
  • Special acknowledgment for collaborative research
  • Celebration of interdisciplinary approaches

Faculty Achievement Honors

The forum will also serve as an opportunity to congratulate and honor our distinguished faculty members who have achieved significant academic promotions or earned advanced degrees. Their contributions to research, teaching, and academic leadership continue to elevate our department's reputation and impact.

  • Recognition of recent academic promotions
  • Celebration of newly earned doctoral degrees
  • Acknowledgment of research achievements
  • Highlighting contributions to academic excellence

Join Us in Celebration

We cordially invite all faculty members, staff, students, and members of the academic community to join us for this momentous occasion. Your presence will not only show support for our graduating students and faculty achievements but also contribute to the vibrant academic atmosphere that defines our department.

Why Attend?

  • Witness groundbreaking student research presentations
  • Network with fellow researchers and academics
  • Celebrate the achievements of our academic community
  • Be inspired by the next generation of chemists
  • Support the culture of scientific excellence

This forum represents more than just an academic event—it is a celebration of the passion, dedication, and intellectual curiosity that drives scientific progress. Together, we will honor the achievements of today while inspiring the discoveries of tomorrow.

Looking Forward

The Chemistry First Closing Forum stands as a testament to our department's commitment to academic excellence and scientific advancement. As we gather to celebrate these remarkable achievements, we look forward to continuing our mission of fostering innovation, supporting student success, and contributing to the global scientific community.

We eagerly anticipate your participation in this inaugural forum and hope it will become a cherished tradition that continues to inspire and celebrate excellence in chemistry education and research.

Join us on Sunday, June 22, 2025, as we celebrate scientific excellence together!
